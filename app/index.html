The Great Gatsby

A study of Color Theory in the Novel

Green- Gatsby’s original dream and hope, his ceaseless pursuit of his dream and even the corruption of his dream and life.

Gold

richness, happy, prosperous, successful, extremely valuable

"...bewitched to a dark gold"

"On buffet tables, garnished with glistening hors-d’oeuvre, spiced baked hams crowded against salads of harlequin designs and pastry pigs and turkeys bewitched to a dark gold."

"...slender golden arm"

"With Jordan’s slender golden arm resting in mine, we descended the steps and sauntered about the garden."

Gold represents Jordan’s status of near unreachability - she is, despite her flaws in personality, a catch. Jordan possesses beauty, as well as wealth, making her desired and appealing to the eyes, like gold.

"...the golden girl"

"That was it. I’d never understood before. It was full of money—that was the inexhaustible charm that rose and fell in it, the jingle of it, the cymbals’ song of it. .. high in a white palace the king’s daughter, the golden girl. .."

The color gold is used here to describe a stereotype that Daisy seems to fit. The “golden girl” of the “white palace” conjures up an image of a young, innocent girl living with the greatest wealth and protection.

Yellow

The color of gold, which symbolizes money, materialism and high social position.

"...bewitched to a dark gold"

"It was a yellow car,” he said, “big yellow car. New."

Yellow is a recurring motif associated with the inevitable death with a corrupt and lavish heart.

"...two girls in yellow"

"...we sat down at a table with the two girls in yellow and three men.."

In contrast to the golden girl Jordan, her admirers are only yellow.

"...yellow cocktail music"

"The lights grow brighter as the earth lurches away from the sun, and now the orchestra is playing yellow cocktail music"

the richness is only a cover, a short sensation, like the yellow press for the more offensively sensational press.

White

morally unblemished, honorable

"...white palaces"

"Across the courtesy the white palaces of fashionable East Egg glittered along the water..."

White is associated with the old money lifestyle of the ones that live on East Egg.

"... both in white"

"They were both in white and their dresses were rippling and fluttering as if they had just been blown back in after a short flight around the house."

Jordan and Daisy are both viewed as pure and innocent.

"...a white card from his wallet"

"Taking a white card from his wallet, he waved it before the man's eyes."

White contributes to power because in this paragraph Gatsby was pulled over for speeding and by waving this card in the policemans face he was able to leave without any problems.

Blue

melancholy, loneliness, tranquility and fantasy

"...his blue gardens"

"In his blue gardens men and girls came and went..."

Blue is usedas a symbol of poverty. We can also tie into this that as opposed to white collar, blue symbolizes the blue collar working class.

"...blue and gigantic"

"The eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg are blue and gigantic—their retinas are one yard high."

They may represent God staring down upon and judging American society as a moral wasteland, though the novel never makes this point explicitly.

"...a dash of blue paint"

" A damp streak of hair lay like a dash of blue paint across her cheek and her hand was wet with glistening drops as I took it to help her from the car."

Blue here is significant because Daisy's hair clearly is not blue, so it must symbolize a feeling. Her lavender hat combined with this sets the stage with cool colors to foreshadow the day that will turn out to contain tears and disappointments.

Red

violence, danger and rage

"...they stood on my shelf in red and gold"

"I bought a dozen volumes on banking and credit and investment securities and they stood on my shelf in red and gold like new money from the mint..."

While the gold is associated with red here, it means that the worship and obtain of money is closely associated with blood and violence in that age.

"...where new red gas-pumps sat"

"Already it was deep summer on roadhouse roofs and in front of wayside garages, where new red gas-pumps sat out in pools of light, and when I reached my estate at West Egg I ran the car under its shed and sat for a while on an abandoned grass roller in the yard. "

In this sentence, the author not only uses the color “red”, but also uses the word “pool”, which indicates the ending of the story.

Grey

decadence, bleakness, corruption and disillusionment

"...the grey land and the spasms of bleak dust..."

"But above the grey land and the spasms of bleak dust which dri endlessly over it, you perceive, a er a moment, the eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg."

The valley of ashes is a grey place where everything is colored grey.

"...a line of grey cars"

"Occasionally a line of grey cars crawls along an invisible track, gives out a ghastly creak and comes to rest, and immediately the ash-grey men swarm up with leaden spades and stir up an impenetrable cloud which screens their obscure operations from your sight."

Every grey thing in the Valley of Ashes makes people feel depressed, hopeless and afflicted.

"...where small grey clouds took on fantastic shape."

"Wilson’s glazed eyes turned out to the ashheaps, where small grey clouds took on fantastic shape."

This indicates Mr. Wilson’s painful state of mind and his determination to kill the owner of the yellow car.
